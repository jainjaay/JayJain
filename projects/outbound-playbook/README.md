# 📦 B2B Outbound Sales Campaign & Objection Playbook

**Target Industry:** Mid-Market Logistics & Supply Chain (Ontario, Canada)  
**Sample Target ICP:** LogiPulse Canada (B2B SaaS HR & Operational Compliance Platform)  
**Target Persona:** Vice President of Operations / Director of Fleet Logistics  
**Core Objective:** Drive outbound discovery calls with fleet operations leaders by solving driver onboarding compliance bottlenecks.

---

## 📧 Touchpoint 1: Cold Email Cadence (Initial Outreach)

* **Subject Line:** Driver onboarding bottleneck at [Company Name]?
* **Focus:** Highly personalized hook, clear pain point identification, and a low-friction call-to-action (CTA).

> Hi [First Name],
>
> Noticed [Company Name] recently expanded your distribution fleet in Ontario. Congrats on the growth!
>
> Many logistics leaders I speak with find that scaling drivers creates massive compliance bottlenecks, dragging out onboarding by 10+ days and delaying delivery timelines.
>
> LogiPulse automates driver credentialing and shift scheduling, helping Canadian fleets cut onboarding time by 45%. 
>
> Worth a brief 10-minute chat this Thursday to see how we compare to your current workflow?
>
> Best,  
> **Jay Jain**  
> Business Development Specialist

---

## 🔗 Touchpoint 2: LinkedIn Social Selling Script

* **Focus:** Soft touch, value-first networking, establishing industry context without an immediate hard pitch.

> **Connection Request Note (Under 300 Characters):**  
> *Hi [First Name] — Saw your recent post on optimizing driver retention strategies in Ontario. I work closely with logistics leaders in Canada to streamline fleet onboarding compliance. Would love to connect and follow your team’s updates at [Company Name]!*

---

## 📞 Touchpoint 3: Cold Call Objection-Handling Matrix

This framework follows the **Acknowledge $\rightarrow$ Pivot $\rightarrow$ Open-Ended Question** approach to disarm prospect resistance quickly during outbound phone calls.

| Prospect Objection | Underlying Meaning | Sales Rebuttal Script (Jay's Playbook) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **"We already have a vendor / system for this."** | Avoidance of painful system migration processes. | *"Totally understand, [First Name]—most top fleets in Ontario already have something in place like Workday or ADP. We actually don't replace your core HR system; we plug directly into it to automate driver compliance tasks that standard HR tools miss. Out of curiosity, how are you currently managing real-time license verification across your drivers?"* |
| **"Just send me an email."** | Quick brush-off to get off the call. | *"I’d be happy to, [First Name]! I want to make sure I only send information that’s actually relevant to [Company Name]. Are you guys more focused on cutting driver onboarding time right now, or minimizing compliance audit risks?"* |
| **"We don't have the budget right now."** | Lack of perceived value or urgency. | *"Fair enough—budgets are tight everywhere right now. I’m not asking for a budget commitment today. Most ops teams talk to us 3–6 months before their renewal to benchmark pricing and feature gaps. Would you be open to a quick 10-minute look next Tuesday so you have data when budget planning opens back up?"* |
| **"Is this a cold call?"** | Prospect's guard is up due to surprise. | *"It is! I’ll be completely honest with you, [First Name]—I reached out because I saw you manage operations at [Company Name], and I figured you’d either be interested in cutting driver onboarding time or you’d hang up on me. Can I take 30 seconds to tell you why I called, and you decide if it's worth continuing?"* |
